Teaching Philosophy

Applied Learning Through Real-World Projects

I believe students learn best when working on authentic research problems. My courses emphasize hands-on experience with real datasets, encouraging students to develop practical skills directly applicable to research and industry careers in geosciences.

Computational Skills as Core Competencies

Modern geoscience increasingly relies on computational tools. I integrate programming (Python), data analysis, and visualization throughout my teaching, ensuring students graduate with strong technical capabilities alongside domain knowledge.

Collaborative & Open Science

I promote collaborative learning, peer instruction, and open-source tools. Students work in teams, share code via GitHub, and learn reproducible research practices that prepare them for modern scientific workflows.

Research Integration

Teaching and research are deeply connected. I bring current research challenges into the classroom, and student projects often contribute to ongoing research, creating meaningful learning experiences and advancing scientific knowledge simultaneously.
