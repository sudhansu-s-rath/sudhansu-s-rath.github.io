About Me

Exploring urban-climate interactions through high-resolution modeling

Currently pursuing PhD at National Institute of Technology, Rourkela, focusing on urban-induced land use change impacts on localized weather and climate. My research combines numerical weather modeling (WRF), remote sensing, and GIS to understand how urbanization affects local climate patterns, particularly in the context of the Indian monsoon system.

I specialize in high-resolution climate simulations, extreme precipitation analysis, and urban heat island investigations. My current work involves WRF v4.7 modeling for the Phoenix metropolitan area and AORC dataset validation spanning 45 years (1980-2024).
