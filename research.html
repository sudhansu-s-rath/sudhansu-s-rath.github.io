Urban-Climate Interactions Over Indian Region

PhD Research | National Institute of Technology, Rourkela

Ongoing

Advisor: Dr. Jagabandhu Panda

Duration: 2015 - Present

Research Focus

Investigating how rapid urbanization affects local weather patterns and climate in Indian cities, with emphasis on:

  • Urban Heat Island (UHI) intensity and spatial patterns
  • Land use/land cover change impacts on local meteorology
  • Modifications to monsoon precipitation patterns
  • Numerical modeling of urban-atmosphere interactions

Methodology

Numerical Modeling

  • WRF Model (v3.9 - v4.7)
  • High-resolution nested domains
  • Multiple parameterization schemes
  • Sensitivity experiments

Remote Sensing

  • Landsat TM/ETM+/OLI imagery
  • MODIS LST products
  • Land cover classification
  • Temporal change detection

High-Resolution Climate Modeling for Southwest US

Collaborative Research | Arizona State University, Tempe

Active

Location: Phoenix Metropolitan Area, Arizona

Duration: 2024 - Present

Project Overview

Advanced WRF simulations for understanding urban-climate dynamics in an arid environment, focusing on extreme precipitation events and validation of long-term climate datasets.

WRF Configuration

  • Model Version: WRF v4.7
  • Domains: 3 nested (18km → 6km → 2km)
  • Initial Conditions: ERA5 reanalysis
  • Timespan: Select periods from 2014-2024

AORC Validation

  • Dataset: 45-year AORC precipitation (1980-2024)
  • Volume: 6.5 GB re-masked data
  • Focus: JJAS monsoon season analysis
  • Resolution: ~30 km hourly data

Key Research Questions

  • How do extreme precipitation events vary spatially across the Phoenix metropolitan area?
  • What is the accuracy of AORC precipitation estimates compared to WRF simulations?
  • How does urban morphology influence local precipitation patterns in arid environments?
  • What are the long-term trends in summer monsoon precipitation over Southwest US?

GIS-Based Agro-climatic Suitability Analysis

M.Tech Thesis | SRM University, Chennai

Completed 2013

Advisor: Dr. R. Annadurai

Study Area: Khordha District, Odisha, India

Objectives

  • Multi-criteria evaluation of agricultural land suitability
  • Spatial analysis of climate, soil, and topographic parameters
  • Crop-specific suitability mapping using GIS techniques
  • Decision support for agricultural planning

Methodology & Tools

Integrated GIS analysis combining multiple data layers including climate normals, soil properties, elevation, slope, and existing land use patterns.
